Threads of Home

My parents were born in Guyana, and that place lives in our house through stories, food, and music. Even though I grew up in America, I always felt tied to a bigger history.

So, Guyana was once called "British Guiana" under British colonial rule, and it became independent in 1966. That change opened a new chapter, but many families still faced economic pressure and political tension in the years after independence.

Like many Guyanese families in the 1970s, my parents emigrated to the United States to build a more stable future. Their journey is why my life is here, but our culture still carries Guyana in everyday ways, and this can be anything, from what we cook to what we celebrate.
